Service Department Operations Guidelines Drop‑Dead Standard

Prepared for Christy Signs by Steve Skye • Date: August 13, 2025
Christy Signs • Service Department SOP
Generated August 13, 2025
Purpose: Establish clear, standardized procedures for all service work—from order creation to receipt of payment—ensuring any team member can pick up an order and proceed without confusion.

1) Order Types

  • Service Order — Repair, maintenance, or troubleshooting of existing signage.
  • Survey Order — On‑site measurement, assessment, or evaluation for new or replacement signage.
  • Warranty Order — Work performed under manufacturer or Christy Signs warranty terms.

2) Work Order Status

StatusDefinition
Work ScheduledJob assigned to date/crew; materials ready or not required.
Work To Be ScheduledAwaiting scheduling confirmation or dependencies.
Parts OrderedMaterials ordered; tracking ETA.
Parts To Be OrderedMaterials list created; PO not yet placed.
Waiting for CustomerPending client approval, information, access, or payment.

3) “Drop‑Dead” Order Standard

Every order must be complete enough that, if the assigned tech cannot continue, another tech can immediately take over. Required fields:

  • Order Type (Service/Survey/Warranty)
  • Customer Info (name, company, phone, email)
  • Site Address & Access Instructions
  • Detailed Scope of Work (plain language)
  • Photos / Drawings
  • Parts & Materials List (+ status)
  • Safety Notes / Special Instructions
  • Target Completion Date
  • Billing Details / Customer PO
  • Internal Notes (history, prior visits)

4) Operational Hierarchy & Responsibilities

Salesperson

  • Create order with complete details and hand off to Service Secretary.
  • Ensure accurate scope, contacts, and special requirements.

Service Secretary

  • Enter order into system to the Drop‑Dead Standard.
  • Assign initial status; attach documentation/photos.

Service Manager

  • Schedule work; oversee progress to completion and invoice readiness.
  • Order parts/materials; maintain vendor ETAs.
  • Customer updates/coordination; enforce work safety.
  • Oversee truck maintenance program.

Lead Technician

  • Execute field work; ensure team safety and quality.
  • Report milestones/issues to Service Manager in real time.
  • Perform daily vehicle checks (see §5).

Assistant Technician

  • Support Lead Tech in installations, repairs, and surveys.
  • Stage tools, manage hardware, ensure site housekeeping.

5) Vehicle Maintenance Standards Lead Tech Daily

  • Fuel & Oil Levels
  • Tire Pressure & Condition
  • Lights & Signals
  • Brakes, Wipers
  • Safety Gear (cones, harnesses, extinguisher)
  • Ladders & Tools Secured
  • Common Parts Inventory
  • Clean cab & bed; no loose items
  • Weekly: submit maintenance needs to Service Manager

6) Process Flow: Order → Payment

  1. Order Creation — Salesperson gathers scope, photos, and constraints.
  2. Order Entry — Service Secretary records details; status set.
  3. Scheduling & Procurement — Service Manager books crew; orders parts.
  4. Pre‑Job Prep — Lead/Assistant Tech review order; load tools/materials.
  5. Field Execution — Work performed; deviations reported immediately.
  6. Customer Sign‑Off — Capture photos, notes, and approvals.
  7. Final Review — Service Manager verifies completeness & documentation.
  8. Invoicing — Accounting issues invoice; terms applied.
  9. Payment Receipt — Record payment; close order; archive artifacts.

7) Attachments & Artifacts Checklist

  • Before/After Photos
  • Signed Work Acknowledgment
  • Time & Materials Log
  • Part Receipts / POs
  • Warranty Notes (if applicable)
